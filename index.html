BidCraft is a free, single-file tool for VFX supervisors and producers. Tag a script into a shot breakdown, build a department-by-department bid, compare bidding rounds, and plan the crew ramp — all in your browser, fully offline. Your scripts and numbers never leave your machine.

Built for real bidding

Years of script-breakdown and bidding practice, distilled into one lightweight file.

Tag straight from the script

Highlight the words describing an effect and tag with custom VFX categories, complexity levels, reusable assets, and assumption/methodology notes. Highlights link back to their shot.

Spreadsheet-grade bidding

A Google-Sheets-style grid with frozen columns, sequence subtotals, keyboard navigation, № multipliers, producer offsets, day rates and live cost totals — in days or weeks.

Bid templates & bulk actions

Save a bid as a template and apply it across selected rows, or use bulk actions to set complexity, tag, park, flag, duplicate or delete many shots at once.

Crew scheduling

Set start, turnovers, delivery and your own custom milestones, then build a per-department weekly ramp and a support-crew plan with a live budget.

Snapshots & compare

Freeze each bidding round and compare any two — color-coded day and cost deltas by scene, shot and asset, so you always see where the budget moved.

Import & export

Import a script PDF or a shot list from Excel/CSV. Export a four-sheet Excel workbook (breakdown, summary, bid, schedule), CSV, or ShotGrid-ready files.

Private by design

No install, no login, no server, no telemetry. Everything stays in your browser — safe for confidential, unreleased scripts.

Tailor it to the job

Toggle whole modules on or off (budgeting, scheduling), hide all prices for screen-sharing, and switch between dark and light themes.

Yours to keep

Save and load complete project files, or export a reusable studio settings file (tags, departments, rates, presets) to standardise every new show.

BidCraft — Tag the script
Breakdown

Tag the script

Paste or import the screenplay, format it into clean layout, then select the text describing each effect and tag it. Scene auto-fills, shot numbers auto-increment, and each tagged shot becomes a card — with assumptions, methodology, complexity and assets attached.

BidCraft — Plan the crew
Schedule

Plan the crew

Set the start, turnovers, delivery and your own custom milestones — each colour-coded and mirrored as a line on the ramp. Build a per-department weekly head-count ramp tracked against the bid budget, then add support crew with a running cost.

BidCraft — Track every round
Compare

Track every round

Snapshot the bid at the end of each round. When the next cut arrives, compare any two snapshots to see the day and cost deltas — rolled up by scene, then broken down by shot and asset, colour-coded for increases and decreases.
